# Ontario ESL Standards — Reference

This document is the reference source for the placement levels and descriptions
used in the assessment. The plain-language "can-do" strings shown on the report
(in [`src/lib/level-descriptions.ts`](../src/lib/level-descriptions.ts)) are
condensed summaries of the expectations recorded here.

## ESL vs. ELD

Ontario secondary schools have two English-support strands:

- **English as a Second Language (ESL)** — for students who have had the
  educational opportunity to develop **age-appropriate literacy in their first
  language**. This assessment targets the ESL strand.
- **English Literacy Development (ELD)** — for students with **limited or
  interrupted prior schooling**. (Out of scope for the current engine — all
  students are routed through ESL.)

## The five ESL levels

The ESL program is organized by **proficiency**, not grade. Each level maps to a
course code and to the engine's per-strand level (1–6). Level 6 means the
student needs no ESL support and is ready for mainstream courses.

| Engine level | Course | Band | One-line focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **ESLAO** | Beginning | Beginning skills for everyday and essential academic purposes; little to no previous English. |
| 2 | **ESLBO** | Developing | Extends everyday/academic language; structured conversations, adapted texts, short paragraphs. |
| 3 | **ESLCO** | Expanding | Increasing fluency; short oral presentations, authentic + adapted texts, academic vocabulary. |
| 4 | **ESLDO** | Consolidating | Greater accuracy/fluency; seminars, grade-level texts, longer essays and summaries. |
| 5 | **ESLEO** | Transitioning | Full transition to mainstream; complex authentic texts, detailed notes, multi-paragraph essays. |
| 6 | Mainstream | Mainstream-ready | Grade-level proficiency; no ESL support required. |

Note: Ontario pairs **Listening and Speaking** in one competency; this assessment
reports it under the "Listening" strand.

## Level 1 — ESLAO (Beginning)

**Focus:** Adjust to the new environment and build foundational skills for
everyday survival and basic classroom participation.

- **Listening & Speaking:** Comprehend specific information in simple directions,
  instructions, and short presentations on familiar topics, heavily supported by
  visual and contextual cues (following physical commands, pointing to objects,
  ordering pictures). Bridge communication gaps with gestures, miming, or asking
  for repetition.
- **Reading:** Read simple instructions, brief information paragraphs, and
  language-experience stories; demonstrate understanding through hands-on
  activities (following a recipe, sequencing events, matching words and
  pictures). Build vocabulary via sight words, personal word lists, pictures, and
  bilingual stories.
- **Writing:** Write phrases and short sentences to communicate basic personal
  ideas; complete simple forms and short journal entries. Use simple spelling
  strategies (word walls, personal word lists, predictable sound-symbol
  patterns).
- **Grammar (taught in context):** Singular/plural count nouns (*table/tables*,
  *child/children*) and subject pronouns; simple present/past/future, present
  progressive, contractions, imperatives; possessive adjectives, high-frequency
  descriptive adjectives, comparatives/superlatives, basic adverbs of time and
  frequency; simple sentences, yes/no and *wh-* questions, conjunctions (*and,
  but, or, because*); basic punctuation and capitalization.

## Level 2 — ESLBO (Developing)

**Focus:** Extend foundational skills for everyday and academic purposes; handle
a variety of text forms and structured classroom tasks.

- **Listening & Speaking:** Participate in structured conversations on familiar
  and new topics; deliver short oral presentations. Pronunciation: final
  consonant sounds in past-tense verbs (*liked, answered*) and plurals; stress on
  the first syllable of compound words (*backpack, toothbrush*).
- **Reading:** Read a mix of adapted and original texts; extract information with
  graphic organizers (T-charts, diagrams); compare information from multiple
  sources; identify connecting devices (*first, second, finally*).
- **Writing:** Organize information into short paragraphs with a clear topic
  sentence, supporting details, and a concluding sentence. Spelling: plurals,
  prefixes, suffixes, and base words.
- **Grammar:** Inverted word order for questions (*Was he studying?*); negative
  imperatives (*Don't sit there*); compound sentences (*and, but, or, because*);
  direct and indirect speech with no tense change; prepositions with literal
  phrasal verbs (*take off, put away*).

## Level 3 — ESLCO (Expanding)

**Focus:** Develop increasing fluency and accuracy; expand academic vocabulary to
ease the transition into mainstream subjects.

- **Listening & Speaking:** Participate actively in class discussions and
  seminars.
- **Reading:** Study and interpret grade-level texts; understand transition words
  signalling sequence, comparison, and cause/effect. Academic vocabulary via
  semantic word webs, word-study journals, and cognates.
- **Writing:** Write longer formats (narratives, articles, summaries); express
  personal ideas through creative forms (a poem, an imaginary conversation).
  Spelling: dividing words into syllables, familiar logographic symbols.
- **Grammar:** Collective nouns (*team, crowd*), indefinite pronouns (*somebody,
  anything*), relative pronouns (*who, that, which*); simple past of low-frequency
  irregular verbs, present perfect, tag questions (*It's hot today, isn't it?*);
  simple figurative phrasal verbs (*give up, look after*).

## Level 4 — ESLDO (Consolidating)

**Focus:** Use English with greater accuracy and fluency in academic contexts;
more sophisticated comprehension and longer written output.

- **Listening & Speaking:** Comprehend specific information from complex
  directions and presentations, taking notes with written outlines or graphic
  organizers. Pronunciation: syllable-stress shifts distinguishing noun/verb
  forms (*COMbat* vs *comBAT*).
- **Reading:** Critical thinking — evaluate sources (websites, newspapers, videos)
  for authority, reliability, and objectivity. Vocabulary: homonyms, synonyms,
  antonyms, and register differences (*astute, intelligent, bright, smart*).
- **Writing:** Write longer, more complex academic texts (debate outlines,
  autobiographies, newspaper articles); paraphrase using indirect speech and
  *that* clauses.
- **Grammar:** Abstract nouns (*advice, knowledge*), non-defining relative
  clauses, negative information questions (*What doesn't she like?*), modals, the
  conjunction *unless*, and a wider variety of phrasal verbs.

## Level 5 — ESLEO (Transitioning)

**Focus:** Refine language for a full transition to mainstream English and
post-secondary pathways; handle detailed, complex academic tasks independently.

- **Listening & Speaking:** Take detailed notes from group presentations;
  summarize documentaries/news reports; complex reconstructive activities
  (dictogloss). Use pitch and volume for emphasis/emotion; advanced circumlocution
  and repair strategies.
- **Reading:** Synthesize information from multiple complex sources; build
  vocabulary by inferring meaning from context, keeping word-association journals,
  and examining nuances of word choice.
- **Writing:** Create complex academic texts (comparative reports, formal letters,
  coherent syntheses of multiple sources); create cohesion by weaving subordinate
  clauses and advanced transition words.
- **Grammar:** Reciprocal pronouns (*each other*); future progressive, future
  perfect, past perfect progressive; passive voice with modals (*Diamonds can be
  mined in Canada*); paired conjunctions (*not only… but also*); indirect speech
  with the past perfect; commas with defining phrases and subordinate clauses.

## Level 6 — Mainstream-ready

Grade-level proficiency across reading, listening/speaking, grammar, and writing.
No ESL course required; the student transitions to mainstream English.
